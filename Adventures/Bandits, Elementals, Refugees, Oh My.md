# Bandits, Elementals, Refugees, Oh My

## Background

Just over a year ago, the island nation of Wreiboth was shattered by an unknown force, devastating the nation, leaving only a crystaline wasteland in the ashes, and making refugees of the Wreiborn people. As their largest (and nearest) direct trading partner, the city-state of Ventus became an obvious destination for the fleeing Wreiborn survivors, many of whom fled into ships with nothing but the clothes on their backs and the coins in their pockets. Covering the dunes that surround Ventus, vast refugee camps were erected in an attempt to help the Wreiborn to maintain some sense of community while they lick their wounds. 

Ventus, however, has no major industry to speak of, instead acting as a neutral mercantile port, one of the few places in the world where traders from all nations, including the pirate havens on Exdosa, exchange goods. This means that the Wreiborn have little by way of options to build new lives here. This drives the more ambitions, or more desperate, Wreiborn to set out on the arduous journey along The Glass Path, the winding caravan trail that passes through the Venta Desert, across the Toznian border and towards the promise of prosperity offered by Toznos, the capital of the Toznian Protectorate.

Caravans across the Venta Desert are usually a large endeavor, well stocked and even more well guarded. This slow, stacatto exodus of the Wreiborn, however, coupled with the inherent difficulty in policing raw, untamed desert, has created a rare opportunity for less moral people, and banditry, once in heard of in this part of the world, is on the rise.

## Hook

Reports have begun to spread among the Wreiborn refugees of attacks on caravans heading from Ventus to Toznos. Survivors report spirits with horrific visages hounding the caravans, leaving smoking corpses and burned out wagons, with no tracks to speak of. The King in Exile, (Name to be created), ignores the calls that he rally the remains of the Griffon Riders, the Wreiborn royal guard, instead claiming his continued efforts to ingratiate himself with the Merchant Lords of Ventus will do more to preserve the future of Wreiboth than chasing ghost stories in the desert.

## The Setup

The players represent a rare oasis of strength when moving through the refugee camp, and those hoping to get resolution to the dangers faced by the Wreiborn caravaneers will look to them for assistance and protection.
