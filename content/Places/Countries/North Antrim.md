# North Antrim

**North Antrim** is one of two nations that were created following a civil war in the former country of Antrim.

## Geography

### Notable Cities

#### Fallcaster

The capital city of North Antrim, it is both the seat of the government and the locus of commerce.

#### The Crossing

What began as literally just the crossing of two main roads, the city known as The Crossing is a major hub, connecting the trade routes that come from the south heading towards Fallcaster with the pair of sister cities in the north and east, () and () respectively. A mosaic of inns, taverns, and various craftspeople required to support the repair and refit of caravans, The Crossing is a mélange of people from across the world. Permanent residents of The Crossing are as likely to have been Toznian, Wreiborn, or Cindron at birth. Many people enter town as part of a caravan, only to find a home instead.

>[!info]
Crime is a challenge in The Crossing, as a thieves guild known as The Scales, has its headquarters in The Crossing. Residents claim that the governments in both The Crossing and Fallcaster must be getting kickbacks from The Scales, as there is only token efforts to bring the group down. Some believe the group's propaganda that they exist 'to bring balance' to the world, while other contend that the thieves only exist to enrich themselves.

#### Northbridge

North Antrim shares the only border with the Veilwood Dominion, an isolated and insular nation of mages. The border passes through the middle an extremely long strait, with tall, rocky cliff on both sides, and there is a single bridge, built from cliff to cliff at the eastern end of the strait connecting the two nations. To manage crossing and trade, Northbridge was founded on the Antrim side of the bridge, and has grown into a large city.

## Socioeconomics

North Antrim's economy largely revolves around three main pillars: forestry / logging, shipbuilding, and a complex service industry supporting overland trade routes that pass from the south into the Veilwood Dominion.

##### Forestry
North Antrim's landscape is dominated by vast forests of both old growth and carefully managed logged forests. In service to both general use and the country's master shipwrights, the Royal Foresters execute generations long complex planting and cultivation plans. Over the centuries, they have husbanded a variety of Ironwood tree that grows large and strong, in a relatively short time. These trees will reach maturity in 37-40 years, compared to similar varieties that would take over 70 to reach similar size and strength.

These forests are guarded as fiercely as any precious metal mine, and tree 'poaching' is punished harshly.

This focus on forestry and developing the best tree varieties possible has brought the Antrimites (prior to the civil war) in conflict with the Druids who live in the unclaimable lands to the north east, often known as the Druidic Wilds, with the worst of these being referred to as [[#The Savage War| > The Savage War]].

##### Shipwrights
Fallcaster may the only major port in North Antrim, but the term undersells how large it is. All major nations and merchant houses have contracts with the shipyards on Fallcaster, who are known to build the most reliable sailing vessels the world has ever known. Owing greatly to the quality of the lumber that is produced locally, these ships are able to be built larger and sleeker than their foreign counterparts, resulting in ships that are strong and fast, with large cargo capacity.

The Shipyards of Fallcaster are officially owned by the crown, and leased to a collection of chartered shipwright houses. These houses are both metaphorical and often literal, as some of the Wrights can trace their lineage as craftsmen to before the establishment of the throne and the charter system. A fiercely matriarchal group, the houses often collaborate on design improvements, with the agreement that 'a rising tide raises all ships', then nonetheless compete viciously over contracts, rejecting the suggestion of collusion in price setting, despite what their clients may claim.

##### Service Industry
Northbridge, both the city and the physical bridge, is the only access to Veilwood, as the Mages have no port cities, and North Antrim is the only country that borders it. Trade, however, has never been stopped by anything so petty, meaning merchants from the world over, wishing to trade with Veilwood and avail themselves of the magical riches it produces, have to travel to and through North Antrim to do so. Many arrive via the port of Fallcaster, while others travel overland from the south. In both cases, the Antrimites have created an entire industry to making the trip 'comfortable.' Traveling the main roads in North Antrim, a caravan can expect to spend every night in an inn, eating hot meals and enjoying baths each morning. Traveling restaurant carts move between Fallcaster, Northbridge, and The Crossing like ants, ensuring the a caravan's quartermaster is well stocked and underworked for their time in country.

Of course, none of this comes for free, and a caravan master will plan on spending much of their coin on these comfort services while traveling through NA, as North Antrim and Veilwood have a trade agreement that gives the authorities in Northbridge the right to waive export / import duties for goods passing through the border, and it is well communicated that the best way to earn this waiver is by 'participating' in this service industry. The overall expense a caravan will put forth is still less than the duties to be waived, so the hassle and headache of the game is considered worthwhile overall.
