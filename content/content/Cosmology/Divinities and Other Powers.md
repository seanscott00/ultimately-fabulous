On the world of [[../Places/Enderon|Enderon]], there are 2 categories of what would be considered higher powers:
- Gods
- [[./Elemental Powers|Elemental Powers]]

### Gods

In the current era, the civilized peoples of Enderon give reverence to 3 deities:
- [[./The Farmer|The Farmer]]
- [[./The Smith|The Smith]]
- [[./The Weaver|The Weaver]]

These three gods exist in a harmonious balance, supporting each other in their fight to protect the mortal realm from the ravages of the Elemental Powers, but also acting as a check, working together to ensure that none of the three interfere too greatly in the activities of mortals.

#### The Farmer
[[./The Farmer|The Farmer]] represents genesis, growth, and change, and cultivation. They reign over the wild places of the world, as well as mortal efforts to tame those same wilds. Childbirth and harvests are both seen as The Farmer's will made manifest. An individual's wishing to improve one's self would seek the favor of The Farmer before entering university or taking up a new trade. Seasons and storms bend to The Farmer's grand plan.

#### The Smith
[[./The Smith|The Smith]] represents industry, invention, magic, and knowledge. Grand projects and personal endeavors both depend on The Smith's blessings. The patron of all who create new things, The Smith seeks to push mortals to great heights, inspiring technological improvement as well as magical mastery.

#### The Weaver
[[./The Weaver|The Weaver]] represents community, law, order, mystery, and conflict. Crafting the grand skein of life, The Weaver inspires groups of individuals to aspire to greater things by working together. This can be made manifest as either grand societies and laws, as well as terrible wars. The Weaver cares for both bonds and laws held to the strict letter as well as aspirational ideals of good and evil. Being an artist, painting with the pigments of life itself, The Weaver also seeks to inspire all art, both great and small, from the sculptor, orator, and player, to the skilled swordsman, plying their trade in the dance of death.


There is a fourth god, that is not revered, or spoken of above a whisper, known as [[./The Shepherd|The Shepherd]].

#### The Shepherd
[[./The Shepherd|The Shepherd]] represents night, death, and the rest of the grave. The Shepherd is the only of the gods that is spoken of with a human form: that of a old shepherd, sturdy and stern, carrying a crook. The Shepherd does not require veneration, however in all churches and temples across Enderon, regardless of which god the location is dedicated to, there will be a shepherd's crook set, untouched, in a visible corner, both as a reminder and as a comfort. As his job is to guide souls on their final journey to the River of Life, the peoples of Enderon generally have a favorable view of The Shepherd, though they do not try to seek him out, as few wish to draw his gaze until their time.


### Elemental Powers

The primordial powers that created and shaped the universe, the elemental powers are wild and untamed, periodically coalescing into beings with sentience and will. When this happens, they gain the ability to manifest in the mortal realm. It is against this coalescence that the gods fight, though they are not always successful. When this happens, calamity can be the result.

Igni - Fire
Hoar - Ice
Zephyr - Air
Fulmin - Lightning
Terris - Earth
